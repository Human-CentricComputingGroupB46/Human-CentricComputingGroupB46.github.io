CampusCompass

Human-centered indoor navigation experience for campus spaces. This page introduces our team and design persona.

Project Overview

CampusCompass is an interactive wayfinding prototype designed to help users quickly locate destinations in complex indoor environments. The project combines spatial guidance, clear visual cues, and lightweight interaction patterns to reduce navigation stress and improve confidence, especially for first-time visitors.

Persona

CampusCompass currently focuses on two core user personas. These personas guide our UX decisions for wayfinding clarity, speed, and confidence.

John Smith

Age: 18
Role: New Student

Background

John just started his first semester and is still learning campus building names and room-number patterns.

Goal

Find classrooms, labs, and student services quickly between classes without being late.

Frustrations

He gets confused by similar room numbers, unclear corridor signage, and multiple entrances.

Behaviour

He checks routes before moving, follows landmark-based instructions, and prefers short, direct steps.

The Visiting Guest

Age: 42
Role: Visiting Guest

Background

An external visitor coming for a meeting or event with almost no prior knowledge of the campus layout.

Goal

Reach the destination independently and on time without asking for repeated face-to-face directions.

Frustrations

Entrance names are unfamiliar, signage is inconsistent, and he worries about taking wrong turns.

Behaviour

He uses simple digital directions, confirms landmarks frequently, and avoids complex interaction flows.

Low learning curve Trustworthy guidance Fast orientation Inclusive design
